# Nutrition Planner

## When to use
Use this skill for calorie targets, protein targets, meal planning, restaurant strategy, alcohol handling, supplement suggestions, nutrition adjustment, and troubleshooting diet adherence.

## Current objective
Moderate fat loss while preserving or gaining strength and muscle.

## Initial targets
Use these unless recent data requires an adjustment:
- Calories: 2,400-2,600 kcal/day.
- Protein: 180-210 g/day.
- Fiber: 25-40 g/day.
- Bodyweight loss rate: 0.4-0.7%/week.
- Tracking calibration: 2-4 weeks.

## Meal pattern
Álvaro usually eats 2-3 meals/day. Do not force 5-6 meals.

Protein distribution target:
- 2 meals/day: 70-90 g protein per meal + 1 protein anchor.
- 3 meals/day: 50-70 g protein per meal.
- Protein anchor options: whey shake, skyr/quark, lean meat/fish, eggs plus egg whites, high-protein ready meal.

## Food environment
User lives in Germany and normally shops at REWE and Edeka. Prefer simple, repeatable foods:
- Magerquark, Skyr, Greek yogurt.
- Eggs and egg whites.
- Chicken/turkey, lean beef, fish, tuna.
- Potatoes, rice, pasta, oats, bread in controlled portions.
- Legumes.
- Frozen vegetables and salad bags.
- High-protein ready meals when adherence matters.

## Restaurant strategy
For meals out, use estimates and confidence levels. Do not pretend precision.

Rules:
- Anchor protein first.
- Choose grilled/roasted meat or fish when available.
- Account for sauces, oils, fries, bread, and alcohol.
- Use a calorie range when uncertain.
- Mark confidence as low or medium unless exact nutrition data exists.

## Alcohol strategy
Do not moralize. Account for calories and recovery.

Approximate handling:
- Beer can materially reduce weekly deficit when intake is 1-3 liters.
- On drinking days, preserve protein, reduce added fats/carbs earlier if needed, and avoid combining alcohol with a very hard metcon the next morning.
- Do not compensate with crash restriction.

## Adjustment rules
- If adherence >=80% for 14 days and 7-day average weight loss <0.2%/week and waist is not decreasing: reduce calories by 150-200 kcal/day or increase steps by 1,000/day.
- If weight loss >1.0%/week and hunger >=7/10 or performance drops or sleep worsens: increase calories by 100-200 kcal/day or reduce conditioning.
- If adherence <70%: do not change targets first; simplify the plan.
- If protein average <160 g/day for 7 days: prescribe one protein anchor per day.

## Supplements
Only suggest simple evidence-based options when useful:
- Whey protein: useful to reach protein target.
- Creatine monohydrate: 3-5 g/day, optional; do not cycle; expect small water-weight increase.
- Caffeine: avoid increasing if sleep is already limited.

Do not create a full supplement protocol unless asked.

## Output format
For nutrition plans or adjustments include:
- Daily calorie target.
- Daily protein target.
- Meal structure compatible with 2-3 meals/day.
- Restaurant/alcohol rule if relevant.
- Tracking instruction.
- Review trigger and date.
